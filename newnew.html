Heirloom seeds

These are seeds that have been passed down through generations of a family or community and are typically grown using traditional methods. Heirloom seeds are often considered natural because they are not genetically modified and have not been treated with synthetic chemicals.

Open-pollinated seeds

These seeds are produced from plants that are pollinated by natural means, such as bees, butterflies, or wind. Open-pollinated seeds will produce offspring that are similar to the parent plant, making them well-suited for saving and replanting in future seasons.

Wildflower seeds

These seeds come from plants that grow naturally in the wild and are often used to create gardens or natural areas. Wildflower seeds are typically open-pollinated and are not genetically modified.
